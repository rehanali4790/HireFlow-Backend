## Email Type Breakdown

### Application Confirmation
**Purpose**: Acknowledge receipt of application
**Tone**: Welcoming, professional
**Key Elements**:
- Confirmation message
- What happens next
- Timeline expectations
- Contact information

**Industry Variations**:
- Technology: "We're excited to review your application"
- Healthcare: "Thank you for your interest in joining our care team"
- Finance: "Your application is being carefully reviewed"
- Education: "We appreciate your interest in our institution"

### Test Invitation
**Purpose**: Invite to assessment
**Tone**: Encouraging, informative
**Key Elements**:
- Test details (duration, questions)
- Technical requirements
- Preparation tips
- Secure test link

**Industry Variations**:
- Technology: Emphasizes technical skills
- Healthcare: Focuses on competency assessment
- Finance: Highlights analytical abilities
- Retail: Tests customer service skills

### AI Interview Invitation
**Purpose**: Invite to AI interview
**Tone**: Supportive, clear
**Key Elements**:
- Interview format
- Technical setup
- Success tips
- Interview link

**Industry Variations**:
- Technology: Comfortable with AI
- Healthcare: Emphasizes personal touch
- Finance: Professional and formal
- Education: Encouraging and supportive

### Shortlist Notification
**Purpose**: Congratulate on advancement
**Tone**: Positive, exciting
**Key Elements**:
- Congratulations message
- Next steps
- Timeline
- Contact for questions

**Industry Variations**:
- Technology: "You're moving forward!"
- Healthcare: "We're impressed with your qualifications"
- Finance: "You've been selected to proceed"
- Retail: "Great news about your application!"

### Rejection Email
**Purpose**: Respectfully decline
**Tone**: Professional, kind
**Key Elements**:
- Thank you message
- Decision notification
- Encouragement
- Future opportunities

**Industry Variations**:
- All industries maintain respectful, professional tone
- Slight variations in warmth and formality
- Consistent message of appreciation

## Customization Tips

### Matching Your Brand
1. **Choose the closest industry** to your business
2. **Consider your company culture** (formal vs. casual)
3. **Think about your audience** (who are you hiring?)
4. **Preview all email types** before deciding
5. **Test with real applications** to verify

### When to Use "Other"
- Your industry isn't listed
- You operate in multiple industries
- You want maximum flexibility
- You prefer a neutral approach
- You're unsure which fits best

### Switching Industries
You can change your industry selection anytime:
1. Go to Settings > Email Templates
2. Select a different industry
3. Preview the changes
4. Save your selection
5. All future emails use the new template

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- Choose an industry that matches your business

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use multiple industries?**
A: No, select one industry for consistency. All emails will use that template.

**Q: Can I customize the colors?**
A: Currently, colors are predefined per industry. Custom colors may be added in future versions.

**Q: What if my industry isn't listed?**
A: Choose "Other" for a professional, neutral template that works for any industry.

**Q: Can I change industries later?**
A: Yes, you can change anytime in Settings > Email Templates.

**Q: Do templates affect email content?**
A: No, only the design and formatting change. Content remains the same.

**Q: Are templates mobile-friendly?**
A: Yes, all templates are responsive and work on mobile devices.

**Q: Can I preview before sending?**
A: Yes, use the preview feature in Settings to see all email types.

**Q: Do I need technical skills?**
A: No, it's a simple point-and-click interface.
